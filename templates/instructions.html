Instructions

The survey consists of five main sections: Participant Information, Consent Form, Demographics, Instructions and Tasks.

There are four tasks, and for each task you will receive the following:


  1. A series of observations that have been given as input into the system
  2. An interactive visualization tool
  3. Some questions about the system

We would like you to read the inputs to the system, and interact with the visualization tool, then answer the questions.
You may use the visualization tool as many times as you want while answering the questions.
Once you are satisfied with your answers, press the “submit” button and move on to the next task.

There are two types of visualizations you will encounter.

In the first kind, you will see an interactive display of a network.
You can click on the states of the nodes to enter the evidence for the task.
Click again on a state to clear the evidence.

In the second kind, you will see a step-by-step argument explaining how the network arrives at a conclusion.
Click through the numbered tabs to step through the argument. Each step contains some text explaining each step, and a visualization highlighting the nodes relevant for the step.

The Bayesian Network we will be using as an example through all tasks is used for medical diagnosis.
In particular, it is used to determine the chances that a patient has lung cancer, given their symptoms, risk factors and medical history.
